# Uncertainty in Safety-Critical Cyber-Physical Systems

## A Standards-Driven Systematic Literature Review

This repository contains the supporting material for the paper:

**“Uncertainty in Safety-Critical Cyber-Physical Systems: A Standards-Driven Systematic Literature Review”**

Authors: **Ayesha Khan, Atif Aftab Ahmed Jilani, Qurrat Ul Ain, Muhammad Usman**
Affiliation: **NCRA-UAV Dependability Lab, Department of Software Engineering, FAST-NUCES, Islamabad, Pakistan**

---

## Overview

Safety-critical cyber-physical systems increasingly rely on sensing, autonomy, connectivity, and machine-learning components. These technologies introduce uncertainty that can affect hazard control, safety assurance, and certification confidence.

This systematic literature review investigates how uncertainty-handling techniques in safety-critical CPS are transformed into certification-ready evidence across multiple domains, including:

* Automotive systems
* Aviation and uncrewed aircraft systems
* Medical CPS
* Rail systems
* Industrial CPS
* Smart grid and energy CPS
* Maritime and autonomous systems

The review also proposes the **Standards-aware Uncertainty Assurance Framework (SUAF)** to connect uncertainty handling with safety standards, lifecycle phases, evidence artifacts, and certification-readiness judgments.

---

## Key Contributions

* A standards-driven review of uncertainty handling in safety-critical CPS.
* A taxonomy of uncertainty types, sources, techniques, and evidence forms.
* Cross-domain comparison across automotive, aviation, medical, rail, industrial, and autonomous CPS.
* Evidence maturity analysis of simulation, prototype, industrial, and deployed studies.
* The **SUAF framework** for mapping uncertainty outputs to standards-consumable assurance evidence.
* A research agenda for certification-ready uncertainty assurance pipelines.

## Systematic Review Methodology

The review follows established SLR guidelines and includes studies published between **January 2015 and December 2025**.

The selection process included:

1. Search across major digital libraries.
2. Title-based screening.
3. Abstract-based screening.
4. Full-text filtering.
5. Forward and backward snowballing.
6. Quality assessment using predefined QAR criteria.
7. Data extraction and synthesis.

A total of **440 records** were initially retrieved. After screening and snowballing, **45 primary studies** were included in the final analysis.

## Research Questions

The review addresses the following research questions:

* How has uncertainty-handling research in safety-critical CPS evolved over time?
* Which safety standards are referenced across CPS domains?
* What uncertainty types and sources are addressed?
* Which lifecycle phases and techniques are used for uncertainty handling?
* How mature and reproducible is the supporting evidence?
* What gaps and transfer opportunities exist across domains?

## Safety Standards Covered

The review analyzes uncertainty handling with respect to standards and frameworks such as:

* ISO 26262
* ISO 21448 / SOTIF
* DO-178C
* IEC 62304
* ISO 14971
* IEC 61508
* EN 50128
* DO-254
* ARINC 653
* STANAG standards
* Safety case and assurance case frameworks

## Highlights

* Uncertainty handling is strongest in automotive CPS research.
* Aviation and UAS studies show stricter certification expectations.
* ML-related epistemic uncertainty remains weakly supported.
* Simulation and prototype evidence dominate the literature.
* Through-life assurance and operational feedback remain underdeveloped.
